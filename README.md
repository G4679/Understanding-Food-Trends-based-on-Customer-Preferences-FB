# Understanding-Food-Trends-based-on-Customer-Preferences-FB
## Project Overview
This project presents an interactive Lifestyle, Nutrition, and Health Risk Analysis using Power BI dashboards to examine how daily calorie intake, dietary preferences, physical activity levels, and existing health conditions affect overall wellness. The dashboards highlight relationships between BMI patterns, cholesterol levels, meal-wise calorie distribution, and diet adherence to identify key lifestyle-related risk factors. By transforming multiple nutritional indicators into interactive visual insights, the project enables a deeper understanding of how dietary behavior and activity habits influence long-term health outcomes.
## Project Objective
Analyze how physical activity levels influence BMI and daily calorie consumption across different population groups.
Examine the relationship between disease conditions (diabetes, hypertension, obesity) and nutritional intake patterns.
Compare dietary preferences (omnivore, vegetarian, vegan, pescatarian) to understand differences in calorie and nutrient consumption.
Evaluate how meal-wise calorie distribution (breakfast, lunch, dinner) varies across lifestyle and health categories.
Identify whether individuals consume calories within or above recommended targets to assess potential health risk exposure.
## Dashboard Overview
## 1 . Indian Cuisine Analysis 
Focus : This dashboard was designed to focus on three major analytical dimensions,Regional Cuisine Distribution,Dietary Preference Analysis,Cooking Time Complexity by Course Type
## Key insights: 
The dataset includes 255 dishes from 25 Indian states, providing broad geographic coverage of regional cuisine diversity.
Vegetarian dishes dominate (86.88%), highlighting the strong influence of plant-based food traditions in Indian cuisine.
The Western region contributes the highest number of dishes (74), while the Central region has the lowest representation (3 dishes).
Main course dishes require the highest cooking time (33.6K minutes), followed by desserts (22K minutes) and snacks (15K minutes), indicating greater preparation complexity for staple meals.
Some traditional recipes involve extensive preparation, with the maximum cooking time reaching 12.17 hours, while starters require the least cooking time (2.2K minutes).
